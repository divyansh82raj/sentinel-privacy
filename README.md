# Privacy Policy — Sentinel

**Last updated: 09-07-2026**

This privacy policy explains what data Sentinel ("the app") accesses,
how it is used, and what is (and is not) done with it.

## What Sentinel does

Sentinel is a fraud-detection tool that checks SMS text messages for
signs of scams (such as fake bank alerts, phishing links, or fraud
attempts) and warns the user if a message looks suspicious.

## Data Sentinel accesses

- **SMS messages**: with your permission, Sentinel reads incoming SMS
  messages in order to check them for scam indicators.
- **Notifications**: Sentinel uses Android's notification system to
  alert you if a message is flagged as suspicious.

Sentinel does **not** access contacts, call logs, location, photos,
files, or any other data on your device beyond what is described above.

## How your data is processed

**All message analysis happens entirely on your device.** Sentinel
does not have a server, does not connect to the internet to analyze
messages, and does not transmit message content anywhere. The
detection model runs locally using on-device computation only.

## Data sharing

Sentinel does not sell, share, or transmit your message content or any
other personal data to any third party, advertiser, or external
service, under any circumstances. There are no analytics SDKs, ad
networks, or third-party trackers integrated into this app.

## Data storage

Sentinel does not maintain a history or log of messages it has
scanned. Messages are analyzed at the moment they are received and are
not stored by the app beyond that.

## Permissions explained

| Permission | Why it's needed |
|---|---|
| Receive SMS / Read SMS | To scan incoming messages for scam indicators |
| Post Notifications | To alert you when a message is flagged as suspicious |

## Children's privacy

Sentinel is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect
data from children.

## Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted here with
